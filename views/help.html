COMP2110/6110 Web Technology

Assignment 1 Peer Marking

The peer marking page allows you to rate the work of the other students in COMP2110/6110 for Assignment 1. The final ratings given will be used as part of the final score for the assignment.

To login please use your student email address (xxx@students.mq.edu.au) and your Github ID as a password. Of course, if you know someone else's email and Github ID you could vote on their behalf, but that wouldn't be nice. If you think someone has impersonated you, please let me know. I keep full logs of access to this site so can try to track down misuse.

Once you login, you will see a page in two parts. At the top is a marking form, at the bottom is one of the submissions from another student. The submission is embedded in an iframe so it is possible that it will look odd. If something does not look right you should try refreshing the page or clicking the link to view the submission in a separate window.

You are asked to review the submission and provide a mark on three criteria. When you submit your marks, another submission will be chosen for you to mark. You will only be allowed to mark 20 submissions at most.

You are asked to rate the stylesheet on three criteria:

  • Overall design - the design looks good and presents the images in a clear and useable way, text is readable, navigation links are obvious.
  • Originality and Creativity - the design shows some creativity, not just a cut and paste from other websites or page templates.
  • Use of HTML/CSS - the submission made good use of HTML and CSS technology to achieve the design rather than just presenting a minimal solution (Wow, how did they do that == Good).

You are also asked to provide some feedback to the student. Use this to make a comment if you want to say more than the scales allow. Try to imagine what you'd like to hear about your own work. Please don't insult anyone; I reserve the right to filter the comments if needed.

Please give some time to considering your rating.

Cheating If it is obvious that this process has been abused then I won't use the results in marking the assignment. Please respect this process and rate the work of your peers fairly.

The final marks for each student will be based on the marks you provide. We will remove obvious outlier marks and we will moderate the final results to make sure they are appropriate.
